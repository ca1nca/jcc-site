The Nagging Problem of Chronic Pain

Acute pain may feel agonizingly intense, but at least it goes away sooner or later. Pain from a surgical procedure, acute injury, or infection will eventually resolve as the physical problem behind it heals up. But some conditions continue for months, years, or even indefinitely, and the pain they cause lingers as well. You may experience chronic pain due to:

  • Bulging or herniated discs that compress major nerves, causing pain signals to shoot through the body
  • Degenerated or arthritic joints
  • Muscle strain and inflammation related to postural/alignment problems or repetitive motion injuries
  • Muscle spasms that cause recurring tension headaches
  • A chronic pain syndrome such as fibromyalgia or myofascial pain syndrome

Since some underlying causes of chronic pain, such as osteoarthritis, are progressive and irreversible, the goal is to manage that pain as effectively as possible. In other cases, it may actually be possible to alleviate longstanding chronic pain by correcting a structural problem that's pinching nerves or straining muscles. Either way, many sufferers think that they have to turn to drugs or surgery to achieve their hoped-for results. But surgery causes its own share of pain and damage, with no guarantee that the pain you want to resolve will go away. Drugs cannot do much more than tame the worst of your symptoms partially and temporarily.
