Common Causes of Knee Pain

Knee pain can occur in people of all ages and health backgrounds. Some of the most common conditions include:

  • Acute injuries, including ligament sprains, meniscus tears, and muscle strains
  • Tendonitis and bursitis 
  • Osteoarthritis, gout, and other types of inflammatory joint conditions
  • Referred pain from the hip or even the spine (if a nerve is being compressed)
  • Patellar dislocation

We see a lot of these conditions in sports. This includes patellofemoral pain syndrome, runner's knee, and iliotibial band syndrome. Factors such as overweight, medical and family history, overuse, and personal injury can also increase a person's risk for knee pain and dysfunction.

Knee Pain Symptoms

People who complain of knee pain often report symptoms such as:

  • Dull, achy, throbbing, or sore pain
  • Pain may be diffuse throughout the joint or in one focal area (e.g., inside the knee, on the outside of the knee, etc.)
  • A feeling or sensation of clicking, popping, and instability 
  • The knee may occasionally buckle
  • Increased pain when kneeling, going up stairs, rising from a chair, etc. 
  • Swelling and tenderness

There may also be related conditions such as shortened leg muscles (especially the hamstrings, calves, and iliotibial bands), altered knee position, and altered hip flexibility/alignment.
