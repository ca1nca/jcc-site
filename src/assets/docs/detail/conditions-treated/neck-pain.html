Chiropractic Care Manages Neck Pain: No Drugs or Surgery

You do not need to be in a major car accident to sustain a whiplash injury, which is a leading cause for neck pain. Even a minor fender bender at speeds as slow as 5 mph can “whip” the neck backwards and forwards, causing a cervical spine misalignment. When the cervical spine is out of alignment, pressure from the misalignment can compress nearby nerves and muscles, triggering neck pain, headaches, and other health problems. If the RCPM muscle is compressed, for example, this can trigger recurring headaches. Symptoms of a whiplash injury include neck pain, fatigue, dizziness, jaw pain and difficulty sleeping at night.

While neck pain is frequently due to a whiplash injury, poor posture can also be a primary contributing factor. When you hunch over a keyboard to see your computer screen or slump back in a chair with the laptop on your knees, your spine is placed in a “C” position. This pushes the head forward while the hips tip back, causing spinal compression. Now imagine assuming this position for eight hours every day while at work – it’s no surprise that chronic neck pain is one of the most common workplace complaints.
