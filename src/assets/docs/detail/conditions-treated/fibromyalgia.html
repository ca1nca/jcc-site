What Is Fibromyalgia?

Fibromyalgia is a condition in which a person experiences widespread musculoskeletal pain. Research indicates that the condition causes pain sensations to be amplified by impacting the way the brain processes pain signals. The condition can occur to anyone, but it is more common in women. The condition may develop over time or may occur after a particular trauma or significantly stressful event occurs. In all situations, it generally is a heightened sensitivity to pain brought about by unbalanced nerve responses to pain signals.

Symptoms of Fibromyalgia

The symptoms of fibromyalgia can differ from one person to the next. Most commonly they include:

  • Pain that's widespread, often a dull ache that seems to occur on both sides of the body both above and below the waist.
  • Fatigue that's chronic and often due to disrupted sleep from pain or conditions such as restless legs syndrome or sleep apnea.
  • Cognitive limitations such as a foggy mind, trouble concentrating, and difficulty focusing.

Many people with this condition also suffer from other concerns such as irritable bowel syndrome, TMJ, and migraines.
