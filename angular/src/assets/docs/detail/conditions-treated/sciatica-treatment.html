What is Sciatica?

Sciatica is a form of back pain that is related to the sciatic nerve. This nerve is located in the back of one or both of your legs. This nerve is tucked into the muscles of the buttocks, back of your thigh, and down into your foot. If this nerve gets pinched you will suffer from sciatica. Usually you will only experience sciatic pain in one leg or side of your buttocks. 

Causes of Sciatica

Individuals suffering from sciatic nerve pain typically have bone spurs or a herniated disc. These conditions lead to increased pressure and inflammation along the sciatic nerve. Most Common Symptoms of Sciatic Nerve Pain

If you are suffering from sciatica the most common symptoms of sciatic nerve pain are burning, tingling, weakness, or numbness. These symptoms will occur along the buttocks or backside of one or both of your legs. You may have difficulty standing, walking, or sitting because of your sciatic nerve pain.
